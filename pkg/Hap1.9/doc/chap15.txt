  
  [1X15 Words in free ZG-modules[0X
  
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XAddFreeWords(v,w) [0m
  
  Inputs  two  words v,w in a free ZG-module and returns their sum v+w. If the
  characteristic  of  Z is greater than 0 then the next function might be more
  efficient.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XAddFreeWordsModP(v,w,p) [0m
  
  Inputs  two  words v,w in a free ZG-module and the characteristic p of Z. It
  returns  the  sum  v+w.  If  p=0 the previous function might be fractionally
  quicker.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XAlgebraicReduction(w) [0m
  [10XAlgebraicReduction(w,p) [0m
  
  Inputs  a  word  w  in a free ZG-module and returns a reduced version of the
  word in which all pairs of mutually inverse letters have been cancelled. The
  reduction  is  performed in a free abelian group unless the characteristic p
  of Z is entered.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XMultiply Word(n,w) [0m
  
  Inputs a word w and integer n. It returns the scalar multiple n* w.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XNegate([i,j]) [0m
  
  Inputs a pair [i,j] of integers and returns [-i,j].
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XNegateWord(w) [0m
  
  Inputs a word w in a free ZG-module and returns the negated word -w.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XPrintZGword(w,elts) [0m
  
  Inputs  a word w in a free ZG-module and a (possibly partial but sufficient)
  listing  elts  of  the  elements of G. The function prints the word w to the
  screen in the form
  
  r_1E_1 + ... + r_nE_n
  
  where  r_i  are elements in the group ring ZG, and E_i denotes the i-th free
  generator of the module.
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XTietzeReduction(S,w) [0m
  
  Inputs a set S of words in a free ZG-module, and a word w in the module. The
  function returns a word w' such that {S,w'} generates the same abelian group
  as  {S,w}. The word w' is possibly shorter (and certainly no longer) than w.
  This function needs to be improved!
  ::::::::::::::::::::::::
  [10XResolutionBoundaryOfWord(R,n,w)[0m
  
  Inputs a resolution R, a positive integer n and a list w representing a word
  in  the  free  module R_n. It returns the image of w under the n-th boundary
  homomorphism.
